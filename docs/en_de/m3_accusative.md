# M3 — The Accusative · worked example

> Theme: **Mio, das hungrige Monster** — a little monster that eats the whole house.
> The verbs *fressen / essen / sehen / haben / möchte* force every object into the accusative,
> so the joke and the grammar are the same thing.
> Draft for review. When approved → compile into `en_de.json` as 7 quizzes.

## The concept (one plain-English rule)

The **accusative** is the **direct object** — the thing the action happens *to* (ask **Wen?**
or **Was?**). Good news: **only the masculine article changes** → **der → den**.
*die*, *das*, and the plural *die* stay exactly the same.

| Case | der (m) | die (f) | das (n) | die (pl) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Nominativ | der | die | das | die |
| **Akkusativ** | **den** | die | das | die |

ein-words too: **ein → einen** (m); *eine* (f) and *ein* (n) don't change.

## 3.5 · `fill_blank_long_text_reading` — „Mios hungriger Tag" (the big text)

> {1:Mio} ist ein kleines Monster und hat immer Hunger. Heute frisst Mio {2:den Tisch}, dann
> {3:die Lampe} und sogar {4:das Sofa}. Zum Nachtisch frisst es {5:die Hausaufgaben} von Tom.
> {6:Tom} sieht {7:das Monster} und ruft laut: „Mio, nein!" Aber Mio hört {8:ihn} nicht und
> rennt durch {9:den Garten}.

**English (info button):** *Mio is a little monster and is always hungry. Today Mio eats the
table, then the lamp, and even the sofa. For dessert it eats Tom's homework. Tom sees the
monster and shouts: "Mio, no!" But Mio doesn't hear him and runs through the garden.*

| # | marked part | question | case |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Mio | Wer hat Hunger? | **Nominativ** |
| 2 | den Tisch | Was frisst Mio? | **Akkusativ** |
| 3 | die Lampe | Was frisst Mio? | **Akkusativ** |
| 4 | das Sofa | Was frisst Mio? | **Akkusativ** |
| 5 | die Hausaufgaben | Was frisst es? | **Akkusativ** |
| 6 | Tom | Wer sieht das Monster? | **Nominativ** |
| 7 | das Monster | Wen sieht Tom? | **Akkusativ** |
| 8 | ihn | Wen hört Mio nicht? | **Akkusativ** |
| 9 | den Garten | (durch + ?) | **Akkusativ** |

➡ Built-in contrast: the **subjects** Mio/Tom are Nominativ; everything Mio devours is
Akkusativ — and *den Tisch* (m, changed) sits right next to *die Lampe / das Sofa* (unchanged).

## 3.7 · 🔊 `dictation` — der vs den by ear

- „Mio frisst **die** Lampe."
- „Mio frisst **das** Sofa."
- „Tom sieht **das** Monster."
- „Mio sucht **einen** Apfel."

## Help Memory (the teaching panel, shown first)

**Intro:** The accusative is the direct object — the thing the action happens *to* (Wen? Was?).
The only article that changes is the masculine one: **der → den**. *die*, *das*, and plural
*die* stay the same.

**Tips:**
- 🟦 **rule** — Only masculine changes. der → **den**, ein → **einen**. Everything else: no change.
- 💡 **mnemonic** — "**den** with the **m**asculine" — the extra *-n* is for the masculine.
- ✏️ **example** — Mio frisst **den** Tisch (m) … but **die** Lampe (f) and **das** Sofa (n).
- ⚠️ **warning** — After *für, ohne, gegen, um, durch* the noun is **always** accusative.

**Table:** the der/die/das → Akkusativ table above (colour-coded by gender).
